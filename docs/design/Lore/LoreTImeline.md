# Timeline of the Seven Kingdoms

## From the Year 84 AC to 120 AC

---

## KEY EVENTS

**The Northern Rebellion, 79 to 85 AC**
In the year 79 after the Conquest, the houses Fisher of Stony Shore, Ryder of the Rills, and Bolton of the Dreadfort raised their banners against Lord Torrhen Stark, joined by the mountain clans Burley, Knott, Liddle, and Norrey. Their grievances were many. Taxation, winter levies, and the burden of preparation for a season that seemed to never end. Lord Belthasar Bolton had a grievance of his own, for he had been denied the hand of Torrhen's sister. Lady Stark fled Winterfell and sought out Belthasar herself, convincing the lord to change his allegiance and taking him as her husband. In 85 AC, Belthasar put the rebel clan lords to the sword and carried their heads to Lord Stark after crushing what remained of the Fisher and Ryder rebels.

**High Crimes and Misdemeanors, 87 AC**
The Crown and House Hightower conspired to place a young septon from Oldtown upon the crystal throne as the new High Septon. They succeeded. In repayment, the High Septon permitted Prince Aeron to annul his union with Constance Arryn, who had refused to provide further heirs. King Jaehaerys I approved the annulment without Queen Alysanne's consent. The queen deemed it needless, for Prince Aemon still lived.

**The Myrish Bloodbath, 92 AC**
Civil war in Myr spilled across the Narrow Sea onto Westerosi shores. Myrish pirates seized the eastern coast of Tarth and put many to the sword. Princes Aemon and Baelon flew their dragons to answer. Prince Aemon took an arrow through the neck and died on Tarth. His death threw the succession into question at once, for his heir, Princess Haera, was wed and not a dragonrider.

**The Great Council, 101 AC**
Prince Aeron Targaryen was chosen by the Great Council at Harrenhal. The decision soured many lords, and more still were displeased when Aeron named his daughter Rhaenor as his heir.

**The Tourney of Maidenpool, 104 AC**
A grand tourney celebrating the succession of King Aeron I was held at Maidenpool. Ser Gregor Bolling, brother of the late Queen Anne Bolling, defeated Prince Jaehaerys in the melee and was named to the Kingsguard.

**The Sack of Lannisport, 104 AC**
Ironborn reavers fell upon the returning Westerlands host at Lannisport. The lords had counted on Princess Rhaella and her dragon for their defense. She was not there. Many were slain.

**The Lion's Roar, 105 AC**
Lord Tybold Lannister did not wait for Rhaella. He gathered his bannermen from Houses Lannister, Reyne, Hetherspoon, Banefort, Parren, and others, and smashed the Harlaw. Women of House Hetherspoon had been taken during reaving season, and the Westerlands repaid the insult tenfold.

**The Dornish War, 107 to 109 AC**
Dornish raids in the Stormlands grew into open war. Lords Wyman Dondarrion and Thomas Bolling fell in the first major battle. The Crown sent dragons, and the conflict ended at Castle Wyl.

**The War for the Stepstones, 112 to 117 AC**
The Triarchy moved against Westerosi interests in the Stepstones. Prince Nymor Martell sided with the Triarchy. Prince Jaehaerys led the royal forces. His nephew Aerys earned a knighthood in the breach where they slew the Crabfeeder together.

**The Tournament of Summerhall, 118 AC**
Prince Aerys struck down Lord Leopold Beesbury in the tilts and killed the man. What followed was a brawl among nobles, royals, and Kingsguard in the mud. Ser Ryam Redwyne took a mortal wound. Prince Jaehaerys ended the fighting by putting Aerys down with a kick to the chest so hard the prince coughed blood for the next seven moons.

**The Year of the Red Spring, 119 to 120 AC**
Sickness swept the realm, claiming lords and smallfolk by the hundreds. Harrenhal burned, and with it Lord Clement Strong and much of his house, including Ser Jon Strong. The Red Spring reshaped the small council and the balance of power in King's Landing.

## TIMELINE

**84 AC**

Spring came to the Seven Kingdoms, and with it both joy and grief to the house of the dragon.

In King's Landing, Lady Constance Arryn was brought to childbed after a long and troubled labor. The babe that came was a girl, small but alive, and Prince Aeron named her Rhaenor. It was the first living child Constance had given him. Two stillborn sons had come before. That same year, Princess Alyssa, wife of Prince Baelon, fell ill and died. The maesters said the cause had nothing to do with the childbirth, though the timing was cruel enough. Baelon was not seen at court for some time after.

In the Westerlands and the Vale, minor raids were reported. Ironborn reavers tested the western shores whilst mountain clansmen grew bold in the eastern hills. Neither threat amounted to much that year, but the reports unsettled those lords whose keeps stood nearest the trouble.

Lord Corwyn Velaryon, the old Lord of the Tides, died in his bed on Driftmark. After the funeral, his son Cyprion gathered his brothers and set sail on the eighth of his nine great voyages. He left behind a young house and a growing fleet.

In the Riverlands, the widowed Lord Franklyn Tully had taken a new wife from House Frey. The match emboldened his goodbrother, who raised the tolls at the Crossing. Winter had already thinned the granaries, and the increase fell hard on caravans from the North and Vale that depended on the bridge. Furious letters went back and forth for moons. When words failed, Princes Aemon and Baelon flew Vhagar and Caraxes to the Twins. Lord Frey was given a choice. He chose to yield, lost a duel of honor to Prince Aemon, and amended the terms of the crossing to the Crown's satisfaction.

**85 AC**

The young Prince Aeron gained notice at court that year, for he had taken it upon himself to manage the Crown's effort in the matter of the High Septon's succession. Working with Archmaester Vaegon, Aeron maneuvered to place Lord Pearse Hightower's son Benedict, then a young and respected septon, upon the crystal throne. The scheme succeeded, though it did not go unnoticed. Scandals rippled from the Citadel down to each and every household maester's tongue and ear, and the gossip did not fade for moons.

Along the Dornish Marches, minor border skirmishes were recorded in the Stormlands. Nothing that required the Iron Throne's attention, though the marcher lords kept their garrisons sharp all the same.

From the east, Lord Cyprion Velaryon sent a small party back to Driftmark. They carried the body of his brother, who had perished on the voyage. The cause of death was not recorded.

**86 AC**

The troubles at the Crossing had not ended.

Lord Frey raised the tolls once more, this time bearing down on caravans from the North and the Vale. Lord Torrhen Stark sent his brother south to mediate. The brother arrived at the Twins and was slain by Lord Frey's own kinsman during a duel on the bridge, though the Stark man had already yielded. Some called it murder. Others called it the heat of combat. Either way, a Stark lay dead on the Crossing, and that was not something the North would soon forget.

Lord Franklyn Tully raised five thousand men and marched on the Twins. Princes Aemon and Baelon returned with their dragons. Lord Frey looked upon the army and the dragons both and refused to surrender his kinsman, calling the dragonriders' bluff. He wagered, perhaps rightly, that neither prince would burn a castle full of innocents over tolls and one dead northman. A siege began.

Far from the Riverlands, in the Vale, Lady Constance Arryn delivered her second stillborn son and declared she would make no further attempts. Prince Aeron was left without a male heir and unable to produce one, by his wife or by any other woman. He had tried. King Jaehaerys I refused his request for a separation, and so the prince turned to allies in the Faith, both old and new alike, to find another way.

**87 AC**

The new High Septon, the one Prince Aeron had helped place upon the crystal throne, repaid the favor. He granted the prince his annulment from Constance Arryn.

The news traveled fast and the reaction was sharp. Lords in the Vale spoke of marching south. In the Riverlands and the Crownlands, the growing reach of the Faith into matters of royal marriage unsettled many, and there were those who saw in the annulment the first stirrings of something dangerous, a crown that owed too much to septons, and septons who would not forget the debt.

The Crown worked to reconcile with House Arryn. Lady Constance wanted none of it. She refused to remarry. Her father, Lord Rodrik Arryn, turned his energies to war against the mountain clansmen instead, and the matter was left to fester.

At the Twins, the siege ended at last. Lord Frey's garrison yielded his kinsman and the kinsman's wife to the Crown. What justice was dealt is not recorded with certainty, though the peace held.

Lord Cyprion Velaryon, having scarcely returned from his eighth great voyage, set sail at once for his ninth and final expedition across the seas.

**88 AC**

The maesters declared the start of a long autumn.

In King's Landing, the City Watch had fallen into ruin. Its reputation was so poor that merchants refused to walk certain streets after dark, and the Lord Commander was stripped of his post. A young man named Leopold Beesbury, of the wealthy Beesbury family of Honeyholt, was given the command. He set about his work with a will, spending freely from his family's own coffers to rearm the gold cloaks and put the fear of the law back into the gutters and alleys of the capital.

In the Riverlands, the houses Blackwood and Bracken clashed once more, this time over a matter of honor involving a young lady of House Frey. The gossiping grandmothers of the Seven Kingdoms had other words for it, though the lords themselves were not so amused.

**89 AC**

A short and mild winter settled over the realm. From the Iron Islands came rumors that the ironborn had built an enormous fleet, though the reports were vague and the maesters cautioned against alarm.

In the Reach, the lords of Tyrell and Hightower sought to win the Crown's favor by hosting a grand tournament in honor of King Jaehaerys I. The tilts were won by Ryam Redwyne, a knight of growing renown.

That same year, King Jaehaerys I turned his attention to the Riverlands, where the old hatred between Blackwood and Bracken had turned bloody yet again. A skirmish along their border had left men dead on both sides. The king condemned both lords and ordered Lord Franklyn Tully to mediate what the Crown called a disagreement. The Blackwoods and Brackens had been killing each other since before the Andals came.

In the Vale, Lord Rodrik Arryn gathered his brothers, nephews, and other kinsmen and made each man swear fealty to his chosen heir, his daughter Constance.

**90 AC**

A great feast was held in King's Landing that year, and it was a night that none who attended would soon forget. Midway through the evening, dancers, singers, fire eaters, and other performers of great expense burst into the hall to announce the return of Lord Cyprion Velaryon from his ninth and final voyage. The Seahorse had come home, and he came home rich.

King Jaehaerys I seized the occasion. He wed the much younger Princess Haera Targaryen to Lord Cyprion in a ceremony that left some at court uneasy and others delighted. The match bound the wealthiest house on the Narrow Sea to the blood of the dragon.

That same year, the young Princess Rhaenor hatched her egg. The dragon that came forth was Syrax.

**91 AC**

Driftmark had never been wealthier or more powerful. Lord Cyprion Velaryon and Princess Haera held court on their island with all the splendor of the Red Keep itself, and the lords of the Narrow Sea bent more readily to the Seahorse than to the Iron Throne, or so it seemed to those watching from King's Landing.

In the Vale, Lord Rodrik Arryn and his sons were killed fighting the Stone Crows. The loss was sudden and complete. Lady Constance Arryn moved at once to claim the Eyrie over her younger brother, Medrick Arryn, a boy of only twelve namedays. The Crown supported her claim. Some lords of the Vale did not. Houses Hunter and Woodhull, the latter being the house of Medrick's mother, raised their banners in protest, but they stood alone. Lady Constance ordered their arrest and exile. Her younger brother and his mother disappeared before they could be brought to account. They were never found.

**92 AC**

Across the Narrow Sea, the Free City of Myr tore itself apart in civil war, and the fighting spilled quickly onto Westerosi shores. Myrish pirates and sellswords raided the Stormlands, seizing the eastern coast of Tarth and taking Lord Tarth himself as their captive. In King's Landing, brief but bloody skirmishes erupted in the streets. Among the dead was Preston Hightower, the second son of Lord Pearse Hightower, a man well known and well liked among the merchants of the Narrow Sea.

The Crown acted swiftly. Princes Aemon and Baelon flew their dragons to Tarth to break the Myrish hold. The battle was short, for the Myrish had not counted on dragonfire. But the victory came at a terrible price. Prince Aemon took an arrow through the neck and bled to death on Tarth. He was three and forty years old. He left behind a daughter, Princess Haera, and a succession that had not been settled.

Prince Aeron assumed himself to be the natural successor to the Iron Throne. King Jaehaerys I and Queen Alysanne said nothing, not in public, not in private. The silence told its own story.

In the Riverlands, the feud between Blackwood and Bracken took a grim turn. What had begun as a rivalry among boys turned to something far worse when Damos Bracken, still a child himself, beat young Bendry Blackwood to death whilst the older boys fought their own brawl and failed to intervene. Lord Franklyn Tully rode out with his household guard to keep the families from raising their levies. He forced a betrothal between the two houses to bind the peace. Every lord in the Riverlands mocked the arrangement, calling it a future disaster. For a time, at least, the killing stopped.

Further south, Dornish raids spiked in the Reach and the Stormlands. A third Vulture King appeared in the red mountains and won a string of swift victories before the marcher lords could rally.

Princess Haera and Lord Velaryon had their first child that year, a girl named Aelora. The Iron Throne granted the Velaryons two dragon eggs to be placed in the cradles of their children.

**93 AC**

Prince Jaehaerys was betrothed to the eldest daughter of Lord Wyman Dondarrion, a match that pleased the Stormlands and quieted some of the talk about the young prince's prospects.

Damion Velaryon was born on Driftmark, a second child and a son. The birth deepened Prince Aeron's unease. Aemon was dead, Haera's family was growing, and Aeron himself had no dragon and no sons.

That changed later in the year, when Prince Aeron bonded with Balerion, the Black Dread. The old dragon was ancient and vast, the last of the three that had conquered Westeros. The bonding was seen by many as a sign of great destiny. Others kept their opinions to themselves.

In the south, a combined host of Baratheon, Hightower, Tarly, and Bolling men brought the third Vulture King to battle and defeated him. The red mountains were quiet again, at least for a time.

Ironborn raids had grown frequent and severe across the Riverlands, the Reach, and the Westerlands. No single attack was large enough to draw the Crown's full attention, but the toll in ships, silver, and blood was mounting year by year.

In the Stormlands, the great houses sought to bind themselves together against the Dornish threat. House Tarly married into House Bolling, and House Fossoway into House Swann.

In the Vale, Lady Constance Arryn loosed her commanders against the mountain clans with orders to root them out and destroy their strongholds. She rode to several skirmishes herself, armored in plate and mounted on a pale grey steed. The clans were driven deep into the hills. They were not destroyed, but they learned to fear the Lady of the Eyrie.

**94 AC**

Balerion, the Black Dread, died. He was the last of the three dragons that had conquered Westeros, and he perished less than a year after bonding with Prince Aeron. The loss was bitter. Aeron had staked much upon the old beast, and now he had nothing to show for it but grief and a throne room full of men who pitied him, which was worse.

Lord Tymond Lannister proposed a marriage between his house and the royal family. It was a request for aid against the ironborn reavers, dressed in silk and courtesy. He did not say Rhaenor's name aloud, but everyone heard it. King Jaehaerys I and Queen Alysanne had another idea. They wed Princess Rhaella, Baelon's daughter, to the widowed Tymond that same year. The match brought a dragon to the Westerlands. Rhaella was young and fierce and flew Meleys. Tymond was old enough to be her father. It was, by all accounts, a cold union.

That same year, King Jaehaerys I and Lord Franklyn Tully conceived a plan to bring peace to the Riverlands by other means. Nine squires, each the son or heir of a powerful river house, were sent to King's Landing to train together under a single master. The hope was that bonds forged in youth might hold where treaties and betrothals had failed. Ser Leopold Beesbury, Lord Commander of the City Watch, agreed to take them. Cayn Blackwood, Jack Bracken, Frances Piper, Jon Strong, Jorah Tully, Marq Mooton, Tommen Roote, Lucias Frey who was then the hostage of Lord Franklyn Tully, and a lowborn but strong and nimble boy named Qhoryn from the lands around Harrenhal.

**95 AC**

Aethan Velaryon was born on Driftmark, the third child of Lord Cyprion and Princess Haera.

In King's Landing, Princess Shaena Targaryen was born to Prince Aeron and his second wife, the Lady Bolling. The birth was difficult. The babe came into the world sickly and small, and the mother's health suffered for it. Both survived, but the maester's tone was grim.

The Iron Throne brought dozens of noblewomen from across the realm into the Targaryen household to serve as handmaidens, attendants, and guards. Among them was Lady Arianne Dondarrion, who entered Queen Alysanne's service, and Lady Aelinore Hightower, who was placed in the household of Princess Rhaenor.

Summer ended without warning on the tenth moon of the year. The temperature dropped sharply, and the maesters spoke of autumn.

**96 AC**

A cold winter came, and with it came trouble of every sort.

In King's Landing, the Faith's influence over the affairs of the realm had grown visible enough to worry men who paid attention to such things. Septons preached in the streets about the sanctity of royal marriages and the will of the Seven, and the small council took note.

The young noblewomen who had entered the royal household the year before settled quickly into their roles. Princess Rhaenor and Lady Aelinore Hightower grew close, or so it appeared.

Prince Jaehaerys shattered the quiet of the court by revealing that he had fathered a bastard on a common woman. The child had already been born. Septon Barth, the Hand of the King, moved swiftly. He exiled the woman and had the prince arrested. King Jaehaerys I withdrew the charges but ordered his grandson to the Starry Sept to contemplate his failures. Jaehaerys did not go. He chased the woman's trail across the Narrow Sea, leaving behind his duties, his family, and his bastard son. Lord Wyman Dondarrion withdrew the betrothal offer at once. Naturally.

That same year, Lady Bolling died from complications that had plagued her since Princess Shaena's difficult birth. The loss struck Prince Aeron hard. He had now buried two wives and gained only a single living child, a daughter. He had no dragon and no sons.

King Jaehaerys I's health had been failing. The old king leaned heavily on Princess Haera and Lord Velaryon, whose wealth and influence preceded them wherever they went. At last, the king named Princess Rhaenor as his heir. The decision pleased Queen Alysanne above all else.

Grand Maester Elysar died under circumstances that some whispered were suspicious, though the man had been old enough that no formal inquiry was pressed. The Citadel took its time in sending a replacement.

In King's Landing, Ser Beesbury judged his squires ready and held a knighting ceremony. Most accepted gladly. Cayn Blackwood refused, declaring that he kept faith with the Old Gods and would not kneel before the Seven. His companion Jack Bracken, newly anointed, drew his sword in defense of the Faith. Whether he truly meant to strike Blackwood or was only making a show of piety depended entirely on who told the story.

**97 AC**

Unease stirred through the realm. Men spoke in low voices about the growing entanglement of Crown, Citadel, and Faith. Three great powers that had long kept their own counsel now seemed to move as one, and not everyone was pleased by the arrangement.

King Jaehaerys I sought to calm the Vale by appointing Lord Henry Hunter as his Master of Laws. The choice was popular with the mountain lords and with the commons, who had heard tales of Hunter's fair judgments.

That same year, Archmaester Vaegon resigned his position at the Citadel and vanished from Oldtown. No search was mounted. The Crown's silence on the matter told its own tale, to those who cared to listen.

The ironborn had grown bolder than ever. Their raids struck the Reach, the Riverlands, and the Westerlands in equal measure, and no lord on those coasts slept easily. In the west, Princess Rhaella and Lord Tymond Lannister devised a scheme to put an end to it. They lured the ironborn fleet to the coast near the Banefort and caught them there with dragonfire. The victory was total. Dozens of senior ironborn leaders burned or drowned, and Princess Rhaella captured the Greyjoy heir and sent him to King's Landing in chains. The Westerland lords who had fought in the operation were displeased. They had done the bleeding, and the dragon had taken the prize. Lord Tymond seized two dozen ships and, as he put it himself, "all of the gold on Pyke."

**98 AC**

The cold winter ended, and King Jaehaerys I marked the occasion with a grand tournament in King's Landing to celebrate his fiftieth year upon the Iron Throne.

Queen Alysanne planned a festival alongside the tourney, with the quiet purpose of receiving suitors for Princess Rhaenor. None were deemed suitable by the princess herself, though the knights Ryam Redwyne and Bradamar Manning gave the crowd their money's worth, breaking thirty lances between them. Ser Bradamar took the victory on points. Ser Redwyne took it poorly.

In the melee, Prince Jaehaerys returned from across the Narrow Sea and made his presence known in the most direct way he knew. He defeated Ser William Arryn of the Kingsguard at only two and twenty years of age. King Jaehaerys I was impressed enough to grant his grandson the Valyrian steel sword Dark Sister.

The Hand of the King, Septon Barth, died shortly after the tournament. His replacement was Ser Ryam Redwyne, the Lord Commander of the Kingsguard. It was a poor appointment. Ser Ryam lasted nine moons before the king replaced him with Prince Baelon. Some have called him one of the worst Hands the realm has ever known.

Baelon took to the role with more care. He became a constant presence in the Red Keep, with his brother Aeron, still annulled, still dragonless, serving as his steward. As Hand, Baelon paid particular attention to the dragons, the eggs, and the bonds between Targaryen kin and their beasts. It is said he kept a private ledger of every egg, every hatchling, and every rider in the family.

In the south, Houses Baratheon and Tyrell announced an alliance. Lord Denys Baratheon took Ysobel Tyrell as his wife. Trade and mutual defense bound the Stormlands and the Reach together, and both regions prospered for it.

**99 AC**

King's Landing had never been cleaner or safer. The gutters ran clear, the streets were patrolled, and merchants who had once barred their doors at dusk now kept their shops open until the moon rose. The credit belonged to Ser Leopold Beesbury, whose years commanding the City Watch had changed the city. King Jaehaerys I rewarded the man by elevating him to Master of Coin and appointing Ser Jon Strong, one of Beesbury's former squires, to lead the gold cloaks in his place.

Princess Rhaenor noticed the new Lord Commander at once. Jon Strong was young, capable, and came from a powerful house. Whether her interest was political or personal, the court took note.

In the Vale, the mountain clans had been pushed deep into the hills, though their numbers remained strong enough to give the lords pause. Queen Alysanne commended Lady Constance Arryn's work in what would prove to be the Good Queen's last known public appearance.

Princess Gael Targaryen died that year. The grief destroyed the queen. Alysanne withdrew from court and was rarely seen again.

**100 AC**

Queen Alysanne Targaryen died. The realm mourned. In some parts of the Seven Kingdoms, black banners flew for half a year or longer. King Jaehaerys I's health, already failing, grew markedly worse.

Talk of the succession began in earnest, spoken aloud now in the halls of the Red Keep and the courts of every great lord. Prince Baelon was Hand, but what of his heirs? What of Aeron's daughter, the named heir Rhaenor? What of Princess Haera and her dragonless claim? There were no easy answers, and the old king gave none.

That year, a curious scandal touched the royal court. Several women of the household were accused of drinking moon tea after bottles of the substance were discovered wrapped in a Targaryen banner and brought before the king by a servant named Malcolm. Prince Baelon, who might have been expected to investigate, ignored the matter entirely. Two things followed in quick order. Malcolm, who was only nineteen, was elevated to castellan of the Red Keep, and several members of House Strong purchased lodgings at the royal court. By year's end, the moon tea and everything it implied had been quietly forgotten.

Malcolm wed a young woman of House Thorne on the last day of the twelfth moon. The ceremony was small and drew no particular attention at the time.

**101 AC**

Prince Baelon Targaryen died of a burst belly during the first moon of the year. He was the king's last surviving son, and his death left Jaehaerys I with an empty chair at his right hand and a succession that could no longer be ignored.

Lord Aston Hightower was named interim Hand. He wasted no time. Working alongside Prince Aeron, the Lord of Oldtown set about building support for Aeron's claim ahead of the Great Council that would be held at Harrenhal to settle the matter.

Much of the realm's attention, however, drifted toward House Velaryon. Princess Haera had a husband with the largest fleet in Westeros, sons who might one day ride dragons, and a claim of her own through her father Prince Aemon. She was a serious contender, and everyone knew it.

Aeron moved fast. He took Lord Hightower's daughter, Aelinore, as his bride within a fortnight of Baelon's death. Aelinore Hightower, who had served for years as one of Princess Rhaenor's closest companions and most trusted confidantes. The betrayal was not lost on Rhaenor. On the eve of her father's wedding, the princess forced Ser Jon Strong, the Lord Commander of the City Watch, to elope with her. The maesters recorded both weddings as intentional and planned well in advance. Few believed them.

At the Great Council of Harrenhal, over a dozen names were put forward for the succession. Only a handful were taken seriously. Princess Haera pressed the claim of her son Damion. Prince Aeron stood on his own name and his new Hightower bride. Prince Jaehaerys returned from across the Narrow Sea without a trace of his common woman, hoping to claim the Iron Throne for himself.

The dragons circled overhead as the lords cast their votes. Vermithor. Syrax. The rest. When the count was done, Aeron was chosen.

King Jaehaerys I and the High Septon accepted oaths of fealty to Prince Aeron as Crown Prince and, to the shock of every lord in the hall, to his daughter Rhaenor as his named heir. Aeron had calculated that the gesture might buy him time. His new queen was already with child. If the babe was a son, the succession could be quietly amended. Princess Rhaenor would understand. Or so he told himself.

On the final moon of the year, Lady Aelinore delivered a son. They named him Matarys. The king did not amend the succession.

**102 AC**

Princess Visenya Targaryen, daughter of Rhaenor and Ser Jon Strong, was born. The child was healthy and strong.

On Driftmark, the young Aelora Velaryon hatched a dragon from one of the two eggs the Iron Throne had granted her family. The hatchling was called Moondancer, and she was small and quick.

King Jaehaerys I grew weaker with each passing moon. His wits began to wander. He forgot names, confused the living for the dead, and spent long hours staring at empty chairs in the throne room. The maesters tended to him, but there was no remedy for age and grief.

Lord Clement Strong was quietly elevated to Master of Whisperers. The appointment drew little notice at the time.

**103 AC**

Prince Aegon Targaryen was born to King Aeron and Queen Aelinore. It was Aelinore's second son, and the news should have been cause for celebration, but the court's attention was elsewhere.

King Jaehaerys I Targaryen died. He had reigned for over fifty years, the longest of any king since the Conquest. His passing was mourned across the realm, though in truth the man they mourned had been fading for years. His grandson Aeron I Targaryen ascended the Iron Throne as King.

Lord Mooton won the bid to host a tournament celebrating the new king's succession. The planning began at once.

In the Westerlands, Princess Rhaella had remained at Harrenhal ahead of the coming tournament, and in her absence the ironborn seized their chance. They fell upon Lannisport as Lord Tymond Lannister was returning from the east. The harbor burned. Lord Tymond was cut down in the fighting. His son Tybold, a young man of thirteen, inherited Casterly Rock, the ashes of a harbor, and a war.

Princess Rhaella flew Meleys back to King's Landing after Tymond's funeral. She did not return to the Westerlands. The speed of her departure was noted, and not kindly.

That same year, Princess Rhaenor bore another child by Ser Jon Strong, a boy also named Aegon. Two Aegons now lived under the roof of the Red Keep, one the son of the king and the other the son of the heir. The coincidence was not lost on anyone.

Ser Ramsey Hunter of the Kingsguard died of natural causes and was replaced by Ser Cayn Blackwood, formerly of the City Watch.

**104 AC**

All with ears could hear the whispering. Queen Aelinore and Princess Rhaenor were both young and fertile, and every birth, every stillbirth, every rumor of moon tea or difficult pregnancy was picked apart by the court with the full enthusiasm of bored and frightened lords.

In the Westerlands, the young Lord Tybold Lannister held his first feast as Lord of Casterly Rock. His bannermen came, paid their respects, and took the measure of the boy who would lead them. Tybold hired Lord Patrek Parren to oversee the rebuilding of Lannisport's harbor and introduced the lord to his bookkeeper and heir, a sharp young man named Jean Parren.

The grand tourney at Maidenpool drew lords from every corner of the realm. It was meant to celebrate the succession of King Aeron I, but tournaments have a way of writing their own stories. In the final joust, Prince Jaehaerys faced a young knight of only eighteen namedays named Ser Gregor Bolling, brother of the late Queen Anne Bolling. Gregor defeated the prince and named his niece, Princess Shaena Targaryen, as the Queen of Love and Beauty. The crowd was delighted. The Faith approved of the display. Lord Aston Hightower, the Hand of the King, saw an opportunity to mend relations with House Bolling and persuaded King Aeron I to appoint the young knight to the Kingsguard as Princess Shaena's sworn protector. Aeron agreed.

On the same day, or near enough, Prince Aeron Targaryen, the second son of King Aeron I and Queen Aelinore, came into the world. He died within hours. The queen's grief was terrible, but the court moved on. It always did.

In the south, Dornish raiding parties began hitting the Reach and the Stormlands with renewed energy. Armories were pillaged, and the skirmishes grew bolder with each moon.

**105 AC**

Winter fell upon the realm after only four moons of autumn. The speed of its arrival caught many lords unprepared, but Lord Aston Hightower, the Hand, proved his worth. He arranged supply routes, reinforced granaries, and posted men to guard them before the first heavy snows. His foresight saved lives. It is said that the smallfolk of King's Landing raised a toast to the Hand at every tavern on the Street of Flour.

Princess Rhaenor gave birth to another child by Ser Jon Strong, a boy named Viserys.

Prince Jaehaerys replaced Jon Strong as Lord Commander of the City Watch. The appointment puzzled some. Jaehaerys was of royal blood and had proved himself capable, but he was a man of wild temper and uncertain moods. Whether the gold cloaks would bend to him remained to be seen.

In the Westerlands, the ironborn struck again, harder than before. Lord Tybold Lannister did not wait for the Crown to send troops or dragons. He gathered his bannermen and sailed for Harlaw. The assault was ferocious. The Westerlands lords fought with a fury born of years of raiding, burned harbors, and stolen women. They sacked the island, retrieved their captives, and seized every ship and scrap of property they could find. Lord Lannister and his company of lords returned to a hero's welcome. Songs were already being written before the ships touched shore.

**106 AC**

King Aeron I hosted a grand tournament in King's Landing to celebrate the fifth year since his wedding and his heir's both. The court called it a celebration of bliss, though the word did not sit well with those who knew the truth between the king and his daughter.

Prince Jaehaerys entered the lists in disguise, as he was fond of doing. His target was the man across the field, Ser Gregor Bolling, the favorite. They met in the final tilt and broke seven lances before Jaehaerys lost his helm. The two drew swords and fought in the dirt. It was not a clean fight. Jaehaerys grabbed a fistful of dung and hurled it into Bolling's greathelm, blinding the knight. Two more minutes of ugly combat followed before Bolling yielded. Jaehaerys claimed the victory. The crowd roared. The court winced.

Later that year, young Aegon Targaryen, son of Princess Rhaenor, hatched a dragon. The beast's name is not recorded in the accounts that survive.

Princess Alysanne Targaryen, daughter of Aeron and Aelinore, was born. The nursery at the Red Keep had never been so full.

**107 AC**

Prince Matarys, the queen's firstborn son, hatched the dragon Sunfyre. The beast was said to be the most beautiful dragon alive, its scales gleaming gold in the light. With Syrax, Moondancer, and now Sunfyre among the younger generation, and the great Vhagar, Caraxes, and Meleys still living, the Targaryen family commanded more dragonfire than at any time since the Conquest.

The growing number of dragons should have been a comfort. Instead, it sharpened every tension that already existed. Each new bonding shifted the balance of power, and every lord who had chosen a side watched the others for signs of weakness.

In the Stormlands, the Dornish raids that had plagued the marches for years grew into something worse. Armored columns of Dornish lords, some equipped with weapons stolen from the very armories they had pillaged, crossed into the Stormlands in force. The first major battle was a disaster for the defenders. Lord Wyman Dondarrion and Lord Thomas Bolling were both slain. Enemy commanders from Wyl and Yronwood were taken alive, but their capture only encouraged Dorne to commit more men.

**108 AC**

In the Westerlands, Lord Patrek Parren died under circumstances that the maesters called suspicious and that his son Jean called murder. Jean Parren assumed his father's titles and his role as lord architect overseeing the reconstruction of Lannisport's harbor. He was young and sharp and hungry, and he finished the work ahead of schedule.

The Dornish war widened. A Tarly host invaded the Prince's Pass, pushing deep into the mountains despite Lord Fowler's declared neutrality. A siege began at one of Fowler's strongholds, and the assault drove many previously uncommitted Dornish lords to take up arms against the Iron Throne.

Later that year, two dozen Dornish raiders breached the walls of Mistwood under cover of darkness. They killed many of the household, seized the ships in the harbor, and sailed home with their plunder before dawn. The boldness of the attack forced the Crown to act. Dragonriders were sent south to end the conflict.

Princess Martell sued for peace. Whether she feared the dragons or simply wished to save her strength, the offer came too late to stop what had already begun. Princess Rhaella and Prince Jaehaerys joined Lord Baratheon's host and marched on Vulture's Roost. Dragonfire reduced the castle to ruin, and the house that held it was exterminated. The remaining armies made ready for their final collision at Castle Wyl.

**109 AC**

Princess Shaena bonded with the dragon Dreamfyre. The bond came easily, which surprised no one who had spent time around the girl. There was something strange about Shaena, something that the kinder voices at court called ethereal and the crueler ones called simply odd. She had always been more at ease with dragons than with people.

Lord Pearse Hightower died in Oldtown. His son, Lord Aston, the Hand of the King, assumed the lordship and traveled south to settle his father's affairs. A riot broke out in the city shortly after his arrival, and the Hand found himself trapped between his duties to the Crown and the chaos in his own streets.

King Aeron I solved the problem by naming Prince Jaehaerys as the new Hand of the King. The court was stunned. Jaehaerys was many things, but no one had ever called him a statesman. Yet the appointment had a certain logic to it. Jaehaerys was dangerous, popular with the commons, and better kept close than left to wander.

Prince Baelor Targaryen, son of Aeron and Aelinore, was born. Mother and child were both healthy.

Winter ended on the eleventh moon of the year, and Lannisport's reconstruction was completed ahead of schedule on the twelfth. The women of the Westerlands, many of them widows of the ironborn wars, planned a festival to celebrate the rebuilt harbor, the end of winter, and their own survival. It was meant to be a small affair. It would not stay that way.

**110 AC**

The maesters declared a new spring and predicted it might last ten years or more.

House Lannister hosted the festival at Casterly Rock and Lannisport, and what had been planned as a modest regional gathering became something far larger. Lords from across the realm requested invitations. The crowds grew until a proper tournament had to be organized to accommodate them. The royal family itself confirmed attendance.

The festivities opened with a ceremony that caught many off guard. Prince Jaehaerys, now Hand of the King, declared before the assembled lords that King Aeron I was bound to the dragon Vermithor. Whether the bonding was true or staged, it was a statement of power. Aeron I had a dragon now, at least in name, and no lord present was willing to test the claim.

In the tilts, Ser Willam Westerling narrowly defeated the freshly knighted Ser Roland Banefort. In the melee, Ser Jack Bracken, whom the men of the Riverlands had taken to calling "Blackjack," fought through every challenger put before him, including his old rival Ser Cayn Blackwood. Bracken took the victory. Blackwood took his defeat with the grim silence his house was known for.

Princess Daenerys Targaryen, daughter of Rhaenor and Jon Strong, was born during the festivities. She was Rhaenor's fifth child, and by the count of the nursery, the heir had the queen well outpaced.

**111 AC**

In King's Landing, the Iron Throne hosted a display of naval power. The realm's greatest admirals sailed their fleets through Blackwater Bay in mock battle, a show of strength against the alliances forming across the Narrow Sea that threatened Westerosi trade. In an upset that delighted the crowds, Ser Jorgen Redwyne defeated Lord Cyprion Velaryon in the final sail. The Lord of the Tides accepted his defeat with grace, or at least the appearance of it.

In the Vale, Lady Constance Arryn held a tournament celebrating twenty years of her rule. Of the royal family, only Princess Rhaenor and her household attended. The tilts were fair and orderly until the final round, when Sers Ramsey Hunter and Gowen Corbray met in a clash of terrible violence.

**112 AC**

War came again.

The Triarchy, that alliance of Lys, Myr, and Tyrosh that had long contested the trade routes of the Narrow Sea, moved against the Stepstones. The Crown declared war and rallied its strength. Houses Targaryen and Velaryon formed the core of the royal fleet, reinforced by men from the Crownlands, the Stormlands, and the Reach.

Much to the Crown's surprise, Prince Nymor Martell of Dorne declared for the Triarchy. Dorne had its own reasons. The Stepstones commanded the southern approaches to the Narrow Sea, and Martell had no desire to see them fall under the Iron Throne's hand.

Late that summer, fierce storms battered the Narrow Sea and Shipbreaker Bay. Tidal waves swallowed ships whole. Sailors who survived the storms swore they had seen krakens stirring in the deep, though the maesters put little stock in such tales.

**113 AC**

King Aeron I declared that he would fly Vermithor to the Stepstones himself.

The decision proved to be a catastrophe. As the king attempted to mount the dragon, Vermithor killed two of his Kingsguard. Aeron got into the saddle all the same, and the beast took to the air. What followed was not a flight but a struggle. Vermithor would not be guided. The dragon twisted and rolled and climbed until it was little more than a speck against the clouds, and then it dove. King Aeron I struck the water from a hundred meters up. Those who saw it said he made no splash at all, so fast was the fall.

Princess Rhaenor flew Syrax out to retrieve him. She found her father floating in the sea, broken but alive. He had shattered every bone in his body save his skull and his jaw. The maesters could not explain how he survived. The recovery would be long, if it came at all.

The war went on without the king. Lord Velaryon pressed the fight in the Narrow Sea, and the Crown sent its land forces under Prince Jaehaerys, who commanded a company of fighters that had earned a hard reputation on the Stepstones.

Lord Clement Strong was named Hand of the King. Queen Aelinore objected. She wanted her father Lord Aston returned to the position, but the council would not have it. As consolation, Malcolm, the castellan of the Red Keep, was elevated to Master of Whisperers on the queen's recommendation.

**114 AC**

A long winter set in, complicating everything. Supply routes to the Stepstones froze or became impassable. Ships that had sailed easily in autumn now fought through ice and gales.

Lord Borys Thorne and his sons set out with a supply fleet to bring food and provisions to the soldiers on the islands. They were intercepted by Myrish pirates and killed, every one of them. The loss left House Thorne in the hands of Lord Borys's daughters, who proved more capable than many had expected.

In the south, House Tarly led a ferocious assault into the Prince's Pass, pressing the Dornish hard and seizing ground that had been contested for years.

**115 AC**

Prince Jaehaerys defeated the Crabfeeder.

The details of the victory were the stuff of songs before the blood had dried. Jaehaerys led the assault on the Crabfeeder's stronghold himself, and his nephew Prince Aerys fought beside him. Aerys was only a squire, but he fought well enough in the breach that Jaehaerys decided the boy had earned his spurs. The prince was no knight himself, as everyone knew, and so he ordered Ser Nichlas Celtigar, the heir of Claw Isle, to perform the ceremony in his stead.

The Crabfeeder's death ended Dornish support for the Triarchy. Prince Nymor Martell pulled his men back across the mountains, and the alliance of three cities found itself fighting alone.

When asked what titles Aerys might claim from the victory, Jaehaerys grinned and told him he could call himself the King of the Stepstones. Whether the prince meant it in jest or in earnest, no one was entirely sure. Aerys, for his part, seemed to take the title seriously.

**116 AC**

The war ground on. Targaryen, Velaryon, Celtigar, Baratheon, and Rosby forces held the Stepstones against repeated attempts by the Triarchy to retake them. The fighting was hard but no longer desperate. The issue had been decided at the Crabfeeder's stronghold, and what remained was a matter of holding what had been won.

Ser Ronnel Rosby earned himself a certain fame that year. A drinking song made the rounds of every tavern from Duskendale to Driftmark, telling how the knight had chopped two heads off with a single swing of his greatsword. Whether the song was true or not, men bought him ale on the strength of it, and that was good enough for Rosby.

In King's Landing, Malcolm, the Master of Whisperers, uncovered a conspiracy to remove the aging Lord Commander of the Kingsguard, Ser Ryam Redwyne.

**117 AC**

The War for the Stepstones ended. There were still minor troubles along the coasts of the Stormlands and the Dornish marches, but the Triarchy had been broken, and the islands were in Westerosi hands.

The royal host returned to King's Landing. Prince Jaehaerys rode at its head, though it was Prince Aerys who announced the victory to the king. He did so in terms that left the court silent. Standing before his father's broken body, Aerys styled himself the King of the Stepstones and the Narrow Sea.

A long silence followed.

Then Aerys laughed and offered to give up the titles in exchange for a proper celebration. The Crown, relieved to avoid a confrontation, agreed. A grand tournament was planned for Summerhall, where the younger Targaryens would compete. The worst of winter was passing, and the mood in the capital was hopeful for the first time in years.

**118 AC**

The tournament at Summerhall was meant to celebrate victory. It would be remembered for blood.

King Aeron I did not attend. His injuries from the fall still kept him confined. The court called the event the Winter's Victory Tournament. Prince Jaehaerys entered as a mystery knight, calling himself the Champion of Winter. The disguise fooled no one, and the crowd groaned rather than gasped when his helm came off.

The trouble began midway through the tilts. Prince Aerys faced Lord Leopold Beesbury, the man who had served two kings as Master of Coin, who had rebuilt the City Watch, who had been respected by noble and common alike for years. Aerys unhorsed him and sent him crashing into the mud. The lord did not rise. He was dead before anyone reached him.

Lord Beesbury's son charged the field with several allies. Within moments, over two dozen nobles, royals, and Kingsguard were fighting in the dirt, already soaked in blood. The sides were unclear. The violence was not. The smallfolk in the stands sat and stared, some said, as if the whole thing were part of the entertainment.

Ser Ryam Redwyne, the Lord Commander of the Kingsguard, killed the heir of Honeyholt but took a wound to the throat. He died of it that night. Jaehaerys fought through the chaos and put Aerys down with a kick to the chest so hard the prince coughed blood for the next seven moons. Ser Gregor Bolling cut through the press toward Jaehaerys, nearly reaching him before Ser Jon Strong threw himself in the way. Strong was a capable fighter, but Bolling overpowered him in a few strokes. It was the Lord Hand, Clement Strong, who finally called for order, and at last the fighting stopped.

No man left that field without blood on him. No man was punished. The red mud and the lost honor were judgment enough, the Lord Hand decreed. Many disagreed. None said so aloud.

In the aftermath, Prince Aerys fled to Dragonstone. Without asking leave of anyone, he went down into the caves beneath the fortress and presented himself to the Cannibal, the oldest and most savage of the wild dragons. He brought bloodied meats and something else besides, or so some whispered. The Cannibal did not kill him. Aerys came back up a dragonrider.

The Lord Hand tried to take action against the reckless prince, but found himself defeated at the small council, which was by then stacked with Hightower kin and allies. Every motion was blocked. Aerys remained free, with a dragon that answered to no one but him.

**119 AC**

Winter ended, and what came after was worse.

They called it the Year of the Red Spring. Sickness swept the realm. In King's Landing, the fever claimed lords, servants, and smallfolk without distinction. Anyone who could leave the capital did so, and quickly.

King Aeron I himself fell ill. He survived, but his condition left him unable to rule for most of the year. Queen Aelinore assumed the regency, and her first act was to dismiss the small council, sending its members back to their own lands. Whether she did this to protect them from the sickness or to gather power into her own hands was debated then and has been debated since.

Before the southern snows had melted, Harrenhal burned. Lord Clement Strong perished in the fire, and with him much of his household, including Ser Jon Strong, the husband of Princess Rhaenor. The cause of the fire was never determined with certainty. The royal court in King's Landing observed a single day of mourning. Black bands worn on the arm and removed before sunset.

Lord Aston Hightower was named to replace Lord Clement as Hand of the King. Lord Jean Parren replaced the late Lord Beesbury as Master of Coin.

Princess Rhaenor withdrew to Dragonstone with her children. She refused all visitors and forbade any further dragon bonding attempts by any member of her household. The princess who had once fought so fiercely for the Iron Throne now wanted nothing more than to be left alone.

Lord Hightower, the Hand, betrothed himself to the young Lady Jyanna Beesbury, a girl of only eight namedays. Her father and brother were both dead. The Hand assumed the regency of Honeyholt until she might come of age. The arrangement was within the law. Whether it was decent was another question, and one that few dared ask aloud.

**120 AC**

King Aeron I made his first public appearance since his fall from Vermithor. He was carried to the throne in a litter and spoke from his seat. His voice was thin and his body was ruin, but he announced his intention to host a festival and tournament before the year was out.

The Red Spring had claimed nigh on a thousand noble lives by the time summer arrived. In the North, the sickness lingered longest. Artys Stark, heir to Winterfell, was among the last to die of it. Lord Torrhen, eighty years old now and burying the last of his line, ordered the young and healthy of his household to ride south and attend the king's festivities in King's Landing. He told them to escape the sickness. In truth, the old lord may simply have wanted them to live.

The realm was changed. The small council had been remade. The Hand was a Hightower. The Master of Coin was a Parren. The heir to the Iron Throne was a widowed princess on Dragonstone who would not answer her own father's ravens. And King Aeron I, broken and diminished, sat upon the throne his grandfather had forged and prepared to host a celebration that no one much felt like attending.

The invitations went out all the same, and the lords of the Seven Kingdoms made ready to answer. Whatever was coming, it would not be quiet.
