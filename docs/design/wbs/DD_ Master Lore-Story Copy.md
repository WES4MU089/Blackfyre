# \[EVENTS\] Timeline

**KEY EVENTS**

**79-85 AC:**

* **Northern Rebellion in 79 AC,** the houses Fisher of Stony Shore, Ryder of the Rills, Bolton of Deadfort, join mountain clans Burley, Knott, Liddle, and Norrey and rebel against Torrhen Stark. Reasons are taxation, levies, and winter preparation. Bolton joins extra because Lord Belthasar was denied Torrhen’s sister’s hand. Prince Aemon Lady Stark flees Winterfell and convinces Belthasar to switch sides and marries him anyway. **In** **85 AC**, Belthasar beheads the clan lords and brings their heads to the Great-Stark after he defeats the Ryder and Fisher rebels.

**87 AC:** 

* **High Crimes and Misdemeanors:** The Crown conspires with House Hightower to elect a young Septon from Oldtown as the new High Septon. It is successful. In repayment, the High Septon permits then-Prince Aeron to annul his union with Constance Arryn because she refused to produce further heirs. King Jaehaerys I ultimately approves without Queen Alysanne’s approval as she deemed it unnecessary as Aemon lived yet.

**92 AC:**

* **Myrish Reaving:** Myrish pirates take over the eastern portion of Tarth, killing many. Princes Aemon and Baelon fly to assist on their dragons. Aemon is killed with an arrow to the neck. This triggers succession concerns immediately as his heir, Haera Targaryen, was wed and not a dragonrider. 

**101 AC:**

* **The Great Council \-** Aeron I Targaryen (*cognate of Viserys I*) is the clear winner in the great council. Considered controversial and soured many lords when he also named Rhaenor his heir.

**104 AC:** 

* **Tourney of Maidenpool \-** A magnificent tourney celebrating the succession of King Aeron I Targaryen is held at Maidenpool. Ser Gregor Bolling, brother of the late Anne Bolling, defeats Prince Jaehaerys in the melee and is named to the Kingsguard.   
* **Sack of Lannisport \-** The Ironborn sack the returning Westernmen host at Lannisport, devastating since they counted on Princess Rhaella for defense. Many are slain in combat.

**105 AC:** 

* **The Lion’s Roar \-** Lord Tybold’s revenge on the Ironborn, he does not wait for Rhaella. Lord Tymond Lannister and Princess Rhaella torch Ironborn ships in 97 AC. Tymond is killed by Harlaw reavers in 103 at Lannisport. Houses Lannister, Reyne, Hetherspoon, Banefort, Parren, and more smash Harlaw after women from House Hetherspoon were taken during reaving season. Westerlands combat hook for those born 90 AC and earlier.

* **Dornish War** begins, lasting until 109 AC. Thomas Bolling and Wyman Dondarrion die.

**TIMELINE**

**84 \-** Spring begins. Princess Rhaenor is born to Prince Aeron Targaryen and Lady Constance Arryn after much difficulty. Princess Alyssa, wife of Prince Baelon, dies shortly after her birth of an unrelated illness, triggering a long mourning period. In the Westerlands and Vale, minor raids from Ironborn and Mountain Clansmen are reported. Lord Corwyn Velaryon dies and after his funeral, Cyprion and his brothers depart for his eighth of nine great voyages. Princes Aemon and Baelon Targaryen fly Vhagar and Caraxes respectively to resolve a trade dispute at the Twins. Lord Benedict Frey loses a duel to Prince Aemon Targaryen, and honorably amends the terms of their arrangement. 

**85 \-** Prince Aeron leads the Crown’s efforts to elect Lord Hightower’s son Benedict- then a young and influential septon- to which Archmaester Vaegon assists. The cabal succeeds despite ensuing scandals echoing from the Citadel down to each and every household maester’s tongue and ear. Minor border disputes are recorded along the Dornish Marches in the Stormlands. Lord Velaryon sends a small emissary back to Driftmark, tragically carrying the body of his brother.

**86 \-** Lord Frey raises tolls on caravans from the North and Vale seeking to cross at the Twins. Lord Torrhen Stark’s son is sent as mediator and is later slain by Lord Benedict Frey- despite yielding during a duel on the Crossing. Lord Franklyn Tully raises an army of five thousand men and is rejoined by the princes Aemon and Baelon once more. Lord Benedict refuses to surrender despite the dragonrider’s threats to torch the Crossing, calling their bluff. A siege begins. In the Vale, Lady Constance Arryn delivers her second stillborn son and refuses further attempts; Prince Aeron is unable to produce a bastard despite trying. King Jaehaerys I famously refuses his request for a separation from Lady Arryn, turning to allies in the Faith- both old and new alike. 

**87 \-** The new High Septon permits Prince Aeron’s annulment and many lords from the Vale intend to march south in protest. The Crown seeks to reconcile with House Arryn over the separation. Lord Rodrik Arryn declares war on the mountain clansmen instead and Lady Constance refuses to remarry. Lord Cyprion makes for his ninth and final great voyage immediately after completing number eight. 

**88-** The ‘Long Autumn’ begins. The City Watch falls to a historically low reputation and the Lord Commander is removed from his station. A young Leopold Beesbury takes command and rules with an iron fist, often making donations from his family’s coffers to re-arm the guards. In the Riverlands, the houses Blackwood and Bracken clash over honor in an alleged ‘love triangle’ with a lady of House Frey- or so the gossiping grandmothers of the Seven Kingdoms so japed. 

**89 \-** A short, mild winter begins. The Iron Islands are rumored to have constructed an ‘enormous’ fleet. Seeking to garner the Crown’s support, the lords of Tyrell and Hightower host a tournament in honor of King Jaehaerys I, won by Ryam Redwyne. King Jaehaerys I condemns the Blackwood and Bracken lords after a particularly violent skirmish along their borders, arranging Lord Franklyn Tully to mediate the so-called disagreement. Lord Arryn makes his brothers, nephews, and other kin swear fealty to his chosen heir: Constance Arryn.

**90 \-** In King’s Landing, a great feast is interrupted by dancers, singers, fire-eaters and other expensive agents of spectacle harken Lord Cyprion Velaryon’s return. Jaehaerys I weds the much younger Princess Haera to Cyprion Velaryon. Princess Rhaenor hatches the dragon: Syrax. Franklyn Tully forces a betrothal between the Blackwoods and Brackens to prevent further strife between the houses; taking the Frey daughter for his own heir. Nevertheless, peace follows the negotiation.

**91 \-** Driftmark’s prestige and glory are ubiquitous with wealth, power, and influence. Lord Rodrik Arryn and his sons are killed fighting the Stone Crows of the Mountain Clansmen. Constance Arryn claims the Eyrie over her elder, male cousin Medrick Arryn- then only 12 namedays- with aid and support from the Crown. Briefly, powerful lords from houses Hunter, House Woodhull, his mother house, attempts a rebellion but raised their banners alone. Lady Constance orders their arrest and exile, though her half-brother and his mother disappear before receiving justice. 

**92 \-** The Myrish Bloodbath begins; a civil war across the Narrow Sea quickly spills onto Westerosi shores. Preston Hightower, the second son of Lord Pearse Hightower with a high reputation with merchants across the Narrow Sea, was slain in the brief but brutal skirmishes in King’s Landing. Lord Tarth is captured in the Stormlands, triggering the Crown to act swiftly against the Myrish invaders who underestimated Westerosi unity and strength in this period. Prince Aemon is slain on Tarth. Aeron assumes himself the natural successor but Jaehaerys I and Alysanne clarify neither in public nor private. Further south, Dornish raids spike in the Reach and Stormlands. A third Vulture King emerges after a few swift successful Dornish incursions. Lord Velaryon and Princess Haera have their first child, Aelora Velaryon. The Iron Throne give the Velaryons two eggs to distribute among their children.

**93 \-** Prince Jaehaerys is betrothed to Lord Dondarrion’s eldest daughter. Damion Velaryon is born, exacerbating Prince Aeron’s concerns over succession since Aemon’s death the year prior. Later in the year, Prince Aeron bonds with the dragon: Balerion. A bulwark of the houses Baratheon, Hightower, Tarly, and Bolling defeat the third Vulture King. Meanwhile, Ironborn raids have become frequent and significant throughout the Riverlands, Reach, and Westerlands. Reaching across to the Stormlands, houses Tarly and Fossoway marry into houses Bolling and Swann, respectively. Lady Constance Arryn empowers her commanders to root and destroy strongholds of the Mountain Clansmen in a massive display of strength, appearing at several skirmishes in plated armor and atop a pale, gray steed. 

**94 \-** Balerion, the Black Dread, dies less than a year after bonding with Prince Aeron Targaryen, devastating the young prince. Lord Tymond Lannister proposes marriage between the two houses in a thinly-veiled request for aid against the reavers in the Sunset Sea, boldly implying Prince Rhaenor. Jaehaerys I and Alysanne have another idea: Princess Rhaella weds the widowed Tymond Lannister later that year with mixed reception from the lords of the realm. King Jaehaerys I and Lord Franklyn Tully send 8 squires- and heirs- of the most powerful houses in the Riverlands with the goal of bonding them young in mutual labor. Ser Leopold Beesbury, Lord Commander of the City Watch, agrees to take the eight young Rivermen as squires: Cayn Blackwood, Jack Bracken5, Frances Piper, Jon Strong, Marcus Tully, Mathos Mooton, Tommen Roote, Lucias Frey who was then the hostage of Lord Franklyn Tully, and a lowborn but large and nimble squire named Qhoryn. 

**95 \-** Aethan Velaryon is born. Princess Shaena Targaryen is born sickly at the expense of mother’s health, though both survive. As a result, the Iron Throne hires dozens of nobles from around the realm to join the Targaryen household as handmaidens, staff and guards. Lady Arianne Dondarrion enters Queen Alysanne’s service. Lady Aelinore Hightower enters Rheanor’s service. Summer ends abruptly on the tenth moon of the year and plummets temperatures into autumn.

**96 \-** A cold winter begins. The Faith’s reputation for influence in politics grows. The young ladies quickly bond with their new companions. Prince Jaehaerys reveals his lover and bastard after its birth; a common woman. Septon Barth exiles the common woman and arrests Prince Jaehaerys. His grandfather, King Jaehaerys I, withdraws the charges and orders him to the Starry Sept. Prince Jaehaerys chases Alaya’s trail over the Narrow Sea, leaving behind his obligations- and his bastard. Naturally, Lord Wyman Dondarrion withdrew the proposal. Lady Bolling dies from complications, devastating Prince Aeron and his family. The King’s health wanes as Princess Haera and Lord Velaryon’s prominence and power precede them, he names Princess Rhaenor his heir. This appeases Queen Alysanne dearly. Grand Maester Elysar dies under suspicious circumstances, though his old age proves enough to overlook it. The Citadel does not immediately provide a replacement. In King’s Landing, Ser Beesbury knights most of his squires and Jack Blackwood refuses, citing his faith in the Old Gods. Newly anointed Cayn Bracken immediately drew his blade in defense of the Seven-Who-Are-One- allegedly. 

**97 \-** Minor dissent ripples through the realm as chatter grows over the converging influence of Crown, Maesters, and Septons. King Jaehaerys I appoints Lord Henry Hunter as his Master of Laws, bolstering popularity in the Vale and with commoners around the Seven Kingdoms. Vaegon resigns from his position as Archmaester of the Citadel and disappears from Oldtown. The Crown’s lack of concern suggests their permission to flee, to those keen enough to notice. Ironborn raids become frequent and significant throughout the Reach, Riverlands, and Westerlands. Princess Rhaella and Tymond Lannister arrange a brilliant strategy to lure and douse the Ironborn ships off the coast of the Banefort in dragonfire, resulting in a decisive Lannister victory. Dozens of senior Ironborn leadership are slain and Princess Rhaella captures the Greyjoy heir for the Crown, irritating Westerland lords involved in the operation. Tymond seizes two dozen ships and to quote: ‘all of the gold on Pyke’. 

**98 \-** The cold winter ends. During the Tournament of King’s Landing to honor Jaehaerys I’s 50th year of reign, Queen Alysanne plans an additional festival to receive possible suitors for Princess Rhaenor. While no suitors were deemed ‘appropriate’ by the Princess, the knights Ryam Redwyne and Bradamar Manning broke thirty lances. Ser Bradamar won by points, and Ser Redwyne . Later during the melee, Prince Jaehaerys defeats Ser William Arryn of the Kingsguard at a mere 22 namedays. King Jaehaerys I grants him the Valyrian steel sword: ‘Dark Sister’. The Hand of the King, Septon Barth, dies and is replaced by Lord Commander Ryam Redwyne of the Kingsguard. Ser Ryam is regarded as one of ‘*the worst Hands in Westerosi history*’, lasting a mere nine moons before he is replaced by Prince Baelon. Baelon becomes an ever-present sight in the Red Keep alongside an annulled, widowed Prince Aeron as his steward. As Hand, Baelon pays special attention to dragons, eggs, and bonds between kin and beast in the family. 

**99 \-** King’s Landing is in its cleanest, safest state in decades and the Crown cuts much credit to Ser Leopold Beesbury. King Jaehaerys I elevates Ser Beesbury to the Master of Coin position and designates Ser Jon Strong as his replacement to lead the City Watch, catching Princess Rhaenor’s attention swiftly. The Mountain Clansmen are largely pushed into the hills though their numbers remain strong. Queen Alysanne commends her works in her last known public appearance. Princess Gael Targaryen dies and devastates the Good Queen, cutting the visit short.

**100 \-** Alysanne Targaryen dies, exacerbating Jaehaerys I’s health and triggers a realm-wide mourning, over half a year or longer in many areas. Conversations spark amongst the royal family and small courts over a possible succession issue with Prince Baelor and his heirs. While wearing bulky robes dyed all-black, several women in the royal court are accused of taking moon tea after several bottles are found wrapped in a Targaryen banner and later presented to the King by a servant named Malcolm. Prince Baelon ignores the matter entirely. Two curious events occurred quickly thereafter: Malcolm is elevated as the Red Keep’s castellan, and that many members of House Strong shortly after purchase residence at the royal court. The conversation of moon tea and its implications are all but forgotten by year’s end. 

**101 A \-** Baelon tragically dies of a sudden appendicitis during the first moon of the year. Lord Aston Hightower is named the interim Hand, and assists Prince Aeron enhance his public image ahead of the Great Council of Harrenhal. Meanwhile, much of the realm’s attention drifted to House Velaryon; specifically Princess Haera and her sons. Aeron takes the interim Lord Hand’s daughter, Aelinore Hightower- also one of Princess Rhaenor’s closest companions- as a bride within a fortnight. Sensing deep coordination and betrayal, Princess Rhaenor forces Ser Jon Strong to elope on the eve of her father’s wedding. Aelinore delivers her first child- Prince Matarys- one moon prior to the Great Council of Harrenhal. The maesters record both weddings as intentional, and well-planned.

**101 B \-** At the Great Council of Harrenhal, over a dozen contenders vie for the Iron Throne. Only a few serious contenders emerged: Princess Haera and her son, Damion; Prince Aeron, and even Prince Jaehaerys returned without trace of his common wife in order to claim the Iron Throne. Vermithor, Syrax, and the rest of the dragons circle above as the votes tally. Ultimately, Prince Aeron is confirmed as the heir to the Iron Throne. King Jaehaerys I Targaryen and the High Septon then accept oaths of fealty to Prince Aeron and as a shocking addition: his heir, Princess Rhaenor. 

**102 \-** Princess Visenya Targaryen, daughter of Rhaenor and Jon Strong, is born. Aelora Velaryon hatches the dragon: ‘Moondancer’.  The King grieves his losses but Queen Alysanne most so; becoming steadily weak, frail, and forgetful as each moon passes. Quietly, Lord Clement Strong is elevated to Master of Whisperers.

**103 \-** Prince Aegon Targaryen is born to King Aeron I and Aelinore Hightower. Naturally, the event was overshadowed. King Jaehaerys I dies and is succeeded by his son, the new King Aeron Targaryen, the First of His Name. Lord Mooton wins the bid to host the tournament for King Aeron I’s ascension. Princess Rhaella remained at Harrenhal ahead of the upcoming tournament, the Ironborn sack Lannisport upon Lord Tymond Lannister’s return and he is killed in battle. Tymond’s son, Tybold Lannister, succeeds him as the Lord of Casterly Rock. Meleys and Rhaella return to King’s Landing full-time after Tymond’s funeral. Shortly after, Prince Aegon, son of Rhaenor and Jon Strong, is born. Later that year, Ser Ramsey Hunter of the Kingsguard dies of natural circumstances. He is replaced by Ser Cayn Blackwood, of the City Watch. 

**104 \-** All with ears eagerly whisper about the ‘racing wombs’ of Queen Aelinore, and the King’s ‘first’ heir, Princess Rhaenor. In the Westerlands, new Lord Tybold Lannister is honored in a feast by his new vassals. He hires Lord Patrek Parren to assist in rebuilding the torched harbor of Lannisport, introducing the young lord to his bookkeeper, protege, and heir: Jean Parren. Lord Mooton hosts the realm at Maidenpool to celebrate the succession of King Aeron I Targaryen. To everyone’s surprise, Prince Jaehaerys is defeated in the final joust by Ser Gregor Bolling- of only 18 namedays. Ser Gregor names his niece, Princess Shaena Targaryen, as the Queen of Love & Beauty. The spectacle is viewed as a demonstration of chivalrous love and is celebrated by the Faith. Lord Aston Hightower persuades King Aeron I to appease sour relations with House Bolling by assigning the knight to protect Princess Shaena Targaryen through his Kingsguard; Aeron I agrees and anoints Ser Gregor Bolling. Prince Aeron Targaryen, son of Aeron and Aelinore Hightower, dies hours after his birth. Dornish bands begin raiding the Reach and Stormlands, triggering minor skirmishes though armories are pillaged.

**105 \-** A sudden winter begins after a mere four moons of autumn. Lord Aston Hightower uses his powers as Hand of the King to arrange a series of supply routes, reinforced granaries, and men to protect them before the first snow. His actions are celebrated by noble and commoner alike. Prince Viserys Targaryen, son of Rhaenor and Jon Strong, is born. Prince Jaehaerys Targaryen replaces Jon Strong as the Lord Commander of the City Watch. Emboldened, Ironborn savagely raid the Westerlands and the lords- led by the new, young Lord Tybold Lannister. They sack Harlaw before the Crown could send troops or dragons, retrieving captives, and seizing ships or other property by year’s end. Lord Lannister and his crew of vengeance-seeking lords are celebrated in the Westerlands by lord and common man alike. 

**106 \-** The Iron Throne hosts a magnificent tournament in King’s Landing to celebrate five years ‘of bliss’ since the dual weddings of King Aeron and his heir, Princess Rhaenor. Prince Jaehaerys entered the tilts in a disguise and would chase the likely champion- Ser Gregor Bolling- from the other side of the list. The two faced off in the final tilt, breaking seven lances before Prince Jaehaerys’ helm flung from his head. The two dueled and Prince Jaehaerys gained the underhand by hurling dung into his opponent’s greathelm- forcing a yield after another two minutes of fury. Prince Jaehaerys claims victory. Later that year, Aegon Targaryen, son of Princess Rhaenor, hatches the dragon: XYZ. Princess Alysanne Targaryen, daughter of Aeron and Aelinore Hightower, is born.

**107 \-** Prince Matarys hatches the dragon: Sunfyre. The rapid addition of dragons adds to the tension underlying an otherwise gilded age. In the Stormlands, minor raids escalate into full invasions armored by powerful Dornish lords and their own stolen equipment. Lords Wyman Dondarrion and Thomas Bolling are slain in the first major battle, and enemy commanders from Wyl and Yronwood are captured; further enticing Dorne to support their countrymen.

**108 \-** Lord Patrek Parren dies under suspicious circumstances while overseeing the reconstruction of the harbor at Lannisport. His son, Jean Parren, assumes his father’s roles as lord and lord architect. In Dorne, a Tarly host invades the Prince’s Pass despite Lord Fowler’s neutral stance towards the conflict in the Stormlands. A siege begins and enhances Dornish support. Later in the year, two dozen Dornish assailants breach the walls of Mistwood, capturing their ships and killing many in the family before taking their bounty back to Dorne in the cover of night. The series of events triggers the Crown’s involvement and the dragonriders are deployed to put down the conflict. Surprisingly, Princess Martell sues for peace. Princess Rhaella and Prince Jaehaerys join Lord Baratheon’s host, and march on Vulture’s Roost. The dragonriders sack the castle and exterminate the house. The remaining armies prepare to finally collide at Castle Wyl.

**109 \-** Princess Shaena bonds with the dragon: Dreamfyre.  Lord Pearse Hightower dies, though Lord Hand Aston assumes both duties for a few moons until a riot breaks out in Oldtown and requires his attention. King Aeron I names Prince Jaehaerys Targaryen to be the Hand of the King, to the shock of the court. Prince Aemon Targaryen, daughter of Aeron and Aelinore Hightower, is born. Winter ends on the eleventh moon of the year, and Lannisport’s reconstruction ends ahead of schedule on the year’s twelfth and final moon. A minor tournament is planned by women, widows, and other Westernmen women to celebrate their victory against the Ironborn, the end of winter, and of course: the refaced harbor at Lannisport.

**110 \-** Spring is declared and the maesters proclaim its potential to last ten years. House Lannister kicks off the minor festival at Casterly Rock and Lannisport, though many houses around the realm request an invitation to attend- much to their surprise. The event is massively anticipated and a tournament is planned to accommodate the crowds- even the royal family confirms their trip westerwards. To begin the ceremonies, Lord Hand Jaehaerys declares King Aeron I bound to the dragon Vermithor. Ser Willam Westerling narrowly defeated the  freshly-knighted Ser Roland Banefort in the joust, while Ser Jack ‘Blackjack’ Bracken defeated all of his contenders in the melee- including Ser Cayn Blackwood. Princess Daenerys Targaryen, daughter of Rhaenor and Jon Strong, is born. 

**111 \-** In the Vale, Constance Arryn holds a tournament celebrating twenty years of her reign. Of the royal family, only Princess Rhaenor and her family attend the event. Despite a fair and orderly tilt, the final round proves brutal: Sers Ramsey Hunter and Gowen Corbray

**112 \-** The War for the Stepstones against the Triarchy is declared and much to the Crown’s surprise: Prince Nymor Martell sides with the Triarchy. 

**113 \-** King Aeron I declares that he will fly Vermithor to aid the ongoing battles in the Narrow Sea. The dragon kills two Kingsguard as King Aeron I mounts his dragon, and he is taken on a hellacious flight which ends in his ‘splashless’ plunge 100 meters into the deep. Syrax and Princess Rhaenor retrieve Aeron who had broken all bones but his skull and jaw. A long recovery begins. The Crown sends many of its personal forces to assist Lord Velaryon’s efforts, including an infamous battalion led by Prince Jaehaerys. Lord Strong is elevated to the Hand of the King, despite rebuke from Aelinore who supported her lord father’s return to the role. Malcolm is elevated from seneschal to Master of Whispers by recommendation of Queen Aelinore.

**114 \-** A long winter begins, frustrating the Crown’s effort on the Stepstones and complicating supply routes. Lord Borys Thorne and his sons are killed by Myrish pirates during his attempt to refuel resources and foodstuffs to soldiers attending the war for the Stepstones, leaving his daughters to helm the highly esteemed House Thorne. House Tarly leads a ferocious assault into the Prince’s Pass.

Prince Aerys, a squire, joins Jaehaerys in the breach and impresses his uncle to the point of earning a knighthood. Jaehaerys, not a knight himself, ordered the act to be carried out by Ser Nichlas Celtigar, the heir of Claw Isle. When asked, Prince Jaehaerys permits Aerys to call himself the King of the Stepstones.

Ser Ronnel Rosby earns prestige from a new drinking song, retelling how he chopped two heads with one swing of his greatsword.

Meanwhile in the North, the Red Spring sickness continues, claiming the life of Artys Stark and over two dozen nobles around the realm. Lord Torrhen orders the young and healthy to attend the festivities in King’s Landing to escape the final throes of the sickness. 

# \[ROLES\] Royal Family

1. King **Aeron I Targaryen** is the son of the late Prince Baelon and Princess Alyssa Targaryen, born in 64 AC. Believing splendour is akin to high holiness and mistaking silence for peace, King Aeron I Targaryen inherited much of King Jaehaerys I’s burdens over the future of House Targaryen. Unfortunately, his action’s consequences often outweighed their cited goals. Prince Aeron annulled one wife and allegedly birthed another to death in pursuit of ‘proper’ heirs. Aeron cleverly filled his household staff with influential noblewomen after second wife’s death after a series of troubled pregnancies. A young lordling Hightower was assigned to supervise his household while on his next task: bonding to Balerion.The young prince was forced to sober and still his mind upon his father’s ascension to Hand of the King in 100 AC. Tragically, Prince Baelon died of illness in 101 AC. triggering the Great Council. The charismatic but uncouth Prince kept his look local for a fast bride to produce more sons through his grief; eventually looking no further than amongst his daughter’s companions. Aeron wed Aelinore on the first moon of 101 AC- igniting an urgent rivalry between Lady Aelinore and Princess Rhaenor.

# \[OPS\] Application Operations

Suggestions go here for applications. 

I would really like to see applicants focus on their political opinions in character rather than their backstory. We know most of the main cast’s backstory. We don’t need a reinterpretation of what we just wrote. What we need to gauge is the writer’s ability to create a political opinion without our intervention, and encourage it to manifest actively in roleplay based on these character traits. The goal is not only to cast inclusive, vivid, consistent, and responsible writers in big roles, but ACTIVE rather than REACTIVE writers. 

EXAMPLE QUESTION: Please identify a major moment in the lore where your character is affected directly or indirectly. Compare the situation and outcome to how it may influence your character’s personal wealth, status, or family in addition to any levies, taxes, or other decrees passed by a great lord or monarch. Form an in-character political opinion and demonstrate with a short writing sample how this might play out.
